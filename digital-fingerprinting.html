FAQ

  • What is the difference between electronic fingerprints and ink and roll fingerprints?

    Electronic fingerprints are captured electronically by a digital fingerprint scanner by a technician. Ink and roll fingerprints are captured by traditional ink and roll method in which the technician records rolled impressions of all 10 fingerprints and impressions of all 10 fingerprints taken at the same time (known as plain/flat impressions).

  • I have to submit my fingerprints to FBI for requesting my police clearance certificate/ Identity History Summary Check/PCC from FBI? Can I do it electronically?

    No, electronic fingerprint submission is not available outside US. You cannot submit digital fingerprints to FBI while residing in India. You just need to send the standard FD-258 card to the FBI directly (along with the application form and other required forms) to retrieve your criminal background check certificate/PCC from the FBI.

  • I think that RCMP Canada requires only electronic fingerprint submission and does not accept ink fingerprints taken on standard fingerprint card (C-216C card). What should I do in such a case?

    Kindly note that Electronic fingerprint submissions to the RCMP are not currently available from outside of Canada. Only RCMP accredited companies (who operate only within Canada) are authorized to digitize ink fingerprints and submit them electronically to the RCMP.

    Click here to visit the official website:- (http://www.cic.gc.ca/english/information/security/police-cert/north-america/canada.asp)

    We will record your fingerprints on the standard C-216 C card and then send it to an RCMP accredited company situated in Canada who would digitize your fingerprints and then electronically transmit it to the RCMP Canada.

  • I am required by my employer to submit my digital fingerprints? What should I do in such a case?

    Fingerprints captured electronically by a digital fingerprint scanner cannot be submitted to any employer/agency or police outside India. You must first get your fingerprints recorded by ink and roll method. We will with the help of our Channel partner in the country concerned get those ink and roll fingerprints digitized and then electronically transmitted to that employer/agency or police.

  • What is the role of Channelers in transmitting the fingerprints to the FBI?

    FBI approves the Channelers to receive the fingerprints (taken on FD-258 card) and other required documents and then electronically transmit the fingerprints to the FBI expediting the application process and reducing the wait time to receive the certificate from 12-14 weeks plus mailing time to 10-12 days only. We have a FBI approved Channel partner who will digitize your fingerprints and electronically transmit them to the FBI.

  • Do the FBI approve any Channelers outside US to electronically transmit the fingerprints?

    The FBI only approves Channelers working within US. No agency in India is authorized to submit digital fingerprints to FBI directly. We will record your fingerprints and get them digitized by an FBI approved Channelers who will electronically transmit them to the FBI.

  • Can I use live scan to submit my fingerprints to the RCMP Canada or FBI USA from India for obtaining my background check certificate?

    No. You have to get your fingerprinting done by ink and roll method on the standard card and then we will send it our partners in Canada or USA to digitize it and electronically transmit them to RCMP or FBI.

  • Is it possible for me to submit the digital fingerprints to FBI (US), RCMP (Canada) or any other agency?

    It is not possible to transmit electronically your Livescan fingerprints or digital fingerprints to any agency outside India. No agency allows you to submit digital fingerprints from India. All agencies including any police agency, employment agencies, FBI US or Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP) Canada require you to submit hard copy of fingerprints from India which has to be digitzed within the concerned country.
