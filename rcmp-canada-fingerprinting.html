FAQ

  • What is a police certificate from RCMP Canada?

    A police certificate is a certificate issued by RCMP Canada containing a statement that you have or do not have a criminal record. Police certificates are different in each country and territory. They may be called:
    * Police clearance certificates
    * Good conduct certificates
    * Judicial record extracts
    * Other names.

  • Who needs a police certificate from Canadian Police?

    Any person who is 18 years or older and who has spent six or more months in Canada need to get a police certificate. The period of six months need not be continuous and if you visited, worked or lived in Canada for two months, left for a few years, then returned for four months, that counts as spending six months. In this case, you would need a certificate.

    If your certificate is in a language other than English or French, send it when you apply, along with the original copy of a translation done by a certified translator.

  • When do I need the police certificate?

    * When you apply to become a permanent resident or a Canadian citizen

    * If you are applying as a
    - Visitor
    - Student
    - Temporary worker
    - Visitor

    * If you are applying for
    - Immigration to Canada
    - Immigration to any country other than Canada
    - Visa/border crossing/ foreign travel/ work
    - Name change
    - Pardon
    - Permanent residency in foreign countries (all countries except Canada)
    - Adoption in
    - Canada or in any other country
    - Travelling in a foreign country for instance Visas, U.S. Waivers, Border Crossing
    - Employment in Canada or any other country
    - Any volunteer work

    * If you have any other requirement (read next FAQ)

  • I got a request from CIC to perform the Police Certificate: A certified RCMP criminal record check based on fingerprints. How do I get one?

    If you are living within Canada:-
    Fingerprints must be submitted electronically to CCRTIS. Paper submissions are not processed and are returned to the originator. From July 1st 2014 the Royal Canadian Mounted Police, RCMP, does not accept mailed copies of inked fingerprint cards except under special circumstances.

    If you are living outside Canada:-
    Electronic fingerprint submissions to the RCMP is not currently available from outside of Canada. Only RCMP accredited companies are authorized to digitize ink fingerprints and submit them electronically to the RCMP. Please note that RCMP authorizes companies who operate only within Canada.

    Click here to visit the official website:- (http://www.cic.gc.ca/english/information/security/police-cert/north-america/canada.asp)
